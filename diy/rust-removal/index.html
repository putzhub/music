Rust removal notes

Summary

If rust is really bad then follow these instructions. If rust is mild then you can use these steps as a guide to see what you should do in a worst case scenario, but your goal is to remove all rust with 80-120 grit sand paper, smooth out any removal with higher grit sand paper. Finishing grit would be at 280-400 to finally prepare the area for either filler or paint. You can use spray paint if the area warrants it, but if you attack rust as soon as you see it, you might be just fine with touch up paint and a brush instead.

Sand paper grits roughly correspond to: removal (~40-120), smoothing (~180-400), finishing (>1500)

Instructions

Remove the Rust w/sand paper

  1. Spray rubbing alcohol/adhesive remover to clean. Move plastics out of the way.
  2. Sand starting with 80 grit, working up. Sanding block applies pressure evenly but sander drill attachment is faster.
    • Wear a dust mask!
    • Remove every spec of rust possible to the bare metal, while keeping area small.
    • Don't forget any rust behind the wheel well or hard to see spots.

Filler

  1. Hammer panel flat or to the angles of the body you're working on.
  2. IMPORTANT: Prep spot with rust reformer after cleaning. Once dry, spray undercoating in wheel wells.
  3. Place body patches between the panels in the affected areas.
  4. Clean the area.
  5. Mix the body filler:
    • Mix on a non-absorbant surface like parchment paper. Pour out body filler so that it forms a circle roughly 1.5 inches high of any diameter. Add hardener along the diameter of the circle for the perfect ratio.
    • Mix the filler in a pressing motion. Gather it up and spread it out. Practice if needed on a small amount.
    • Mixing only takes 45 seconds and filler hardens after 5 minutes.
    • Never leave the filler lumped up, spread out evenly to allow heat to escape for more mixing time.
  6. Apply filler to damaged area and press the filler hard into the damage. Ensure good contact and force air out. Meant for strength and foundation.
    • Tip: When done, leave the filler on the spreader to dry for easier clean up later. Bend it once dry to clean.

Sanding

  1. Sand filler in a well ventilated area starting with 40 or 80 grit. Sand filler to the shape of surrounding body.
  2. Mix Gold filler using the same technique as before. Apply over the first layer in a thick layer to allow for sanding smooth, working up to 180 grit sand paper. Sand out any bumps before proceeding!
  3. Use Glazing and Spot putty to fill in any pinholes before moving up to 320 grit sand paper. Wait until dry then spread with spreader.

Paint

  1. Before painting anything blow away all dust in the area so it doesn't get in the paint job. Wear a dust mask!
  2. After the dust settles clean off the area one more time.
  3. Spray 3 layers of primer filler.
  4. To avoid painting on the surrounding, unaffected area use paper taped at the edges without folding, so it remains rounded. Use the rounded paper as a shield instead of painting tape to avoid harsh paint lines. [[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=n4vusY2-rkQ&t=884|Refer to video at 15:00.]] Place garbage bag taped over wheel if necessary to protect wheels.
  5. From here you can paint the car yourself or take it into a shop if you'd prefer for a reduced price.
  6. Sand down the primer in the work area a little bit into the surrounding paint lightly. Smooth out the transition between the primer and paint. Don't press to hard, let the sand paper do the work!
  7. Clean off the area with adhesive remover.
  8. Spray paint the base coat of the car's color. Light even coats, don't spray too thick yet. Then wait til it dries to a matte finish and apply 2 slightly thicker layers.
  9. Once base coat is dry then apply clear coat. Put the can in warm water for a while beforehand to acheive a better spray pattern. REMOVE ALL WATER CONDENSATION BEFORE USING! Apply 3 layers in total. Let cleat coat dry before removing any dust.
  10. Once dry (check paint label for details on how long to wait), clean off the area with soap and water. Soak 1500 grisand paper in soapy water for about 10 minutes then use to sand. Wrap sand paper over a sponge for a wet sanding block.
    • Sand back and forth, not in circular motions! Sand across the body of the work area, not in one spot.
  11. Clean with soap and water. Wet sand with 2000 grit, then 3000 grit then end with 5000 grit the same way.
  12. Polish the paint job to buff it out. Keep the buffer moving if electric to avoid burning and then wipe down the paint job when done.

Congratulations! You did it.
