Timelines

There are types of feeds: Personal, Local and Federated.

Personal

The Personal timeline shows you the posts & boosts from people you follow. Pretty easy. When you first join you'll only see posts from the administrator because you're only following them.

As you make friends and follow people, this will fill up (hopefully).

Local

The Local timeline is the place where everyone who is on my server can talk to each other, like a town plaza. Here's a picture of how it looks now. Depending on how many people are active this is usually a slower moving feed.

Federated

The Federated timeline is a feed of: * everyone who is posting public messages on the Local timeline. * people who who are followed by anyone on the server * boosted posts of the people those people follow * If connected to a relay, everyone on the fediverse

In short, depending on how your admin has configured it: everyone. In my case, we're connected to a relay. However this is not necessary, in which case the community will be much smaller, but maybe you're looking for that.

Notifications

Notifications are given in a feed as well. They update you with any mentions, follows, boosts of posts, favorites. All of it is in one place. You can use the configuration button on the top right to filter what is shown to make your feed more readable.

Searching

Finding stuff on Mastodon is fairly easy. The feeds automatically refresh if you scroll to the top. Hashtags are clickable to search for more posts by. The search bar also lets you search for content.

You can search for users, hashtags and on some servers post contents.

Users

Users are searchable as well. You can either use just the username or the username and the domain name if you're searching for someone particular.

Profile preferences

Click the preferences tab either in the navigation bar or next to your username if your screen looks different than mine to open.

Public profile

Here you can edit your avatar, profile page image header, set a description and change your display name as well. You can choose to make your account private so that only approved people can follow you. Note: This defaults your posts to private, so you'll have to set them to public to be visible on the federated timeline. More on that here.

The profile also includes a table that you can use to set labels and contents for things like indicating which website people can find your music at or even labeling your gender pronouns. It's customizable based on your own interests.

Filtering

Mastodon is about putting control of the feed back into not only community hands, but also into your hands.

One option for this is in the preferences menu under filters. Here you can set posts with keywords to be automatically filtered and from which timeline for how long.

On the timeline if you find someone annoying, just block them, mute them, or even report them, especially if anyone from the federated timeline is harassing other users.

To filter posts directly, click the elipses in the post to either report, block or silence a user. You can even block and entire server if you find most of their posters are annoying or offensive.

Following

In order to follow users click on their name and it wil take you to their page. You'll see their landing page and toots. From here you can click follow in the upper right corner if you want to follow them or you can click the 3 dots to choose a different action.

This is what the public sees when they click on your page.

Posting

In order to post you must either click the compose button in the bottom right corner of the feed you're on or on a reply. If you're using a desktop it should be visible on the left-hand side already.

Privacy settings

Mastodon allows you to set 4 different privacy settings: * Public: everyone can see it (federated) * Unfederated: only local public timeline can see it * Followers: only followers can see (not local or federated timelines) * Direct message: only addressed users get the message

Content Warnings

If you click on CW it will hide the post behind a content warning that will hide your post until it's clicked. This is useful for sensitive subjects or really long posts so that they don't clog up the feed.

Popular content warnings include: * NSFW or lewd * pol (politics) * selfie (eye contenat/no eye contact) * long * meta (regarding mastodon) * abuse (thoughts or stories relating to abuse) * mh (mental health)

Polls

You can start a poll as well that can be set to expire after a while when others will be notified of the results.
